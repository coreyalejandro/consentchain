## Essay 2: Please tell us briefly about an area of technical AI safety work you're currently excited about, and why.

I am currently obsessed with the intersection of **Constitutional AI (CAI)** and **Intent-Based Alignment**.

Specifically, I am exploring how we can move beyond passive "Rule Following" to active "Intent Internalization." The critical failure mode I keep encountering is not that models break rules — it's that they compress and transform human intent during the translation from natural language to execution, without the human knowing it happened. I call this the "Intent Compression Problem."

My research project, "Intention is All You Need" (ITAYN), builds mechanisms to treat human intent as a high-fidelity, auditable input signal that overrides standard optimization shortcuts. The insight came from my time as a Dean: systems fail not because the rules are wrong, but because the *spirit* of the rules is lost in implementation. Students fail not because they're incapable, but because the system mistranslates their cognitive style as deficiency.

I am particularly excited about Anthropic's work on RLAIF (Reinforcement Learning from AI Feedback) as the most promising path toward self-correcting constitutional alignment. I am building toward a "Guardian Kernel" — a fail-closed audit layer that ensures no model action is taken without a forensic check against the governing constitution. This is not novel as a concept; it is novel as an *engineering discipline* applied to agentic systems.

My specific contribution: extending CAI techniques to handle neuro-diverse communication patterns. Safety cannot be "safety for the average user." It must be safety for the edge-case human — like me.
